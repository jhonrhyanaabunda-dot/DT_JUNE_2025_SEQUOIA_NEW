02The redesign

What's actually new on the 2025 Sequoia.

The current Sequoia rides on Toyota's TNGA-F body-on-frame platform, the same architecture under the Tundra and Land Cruiser, a meaningful change from the long-running previous generation. Here's what matters when you're standing next to one on the lot.

PowertrainHybrid only

One powertrain, and it's a hybrid

i-FORCE MAX: twin-turbo 3.4L V6 + electric motor. Combined 437 horsepower and 583 lb-ft of torque. There is no non-hybrid gas-only Sequoia anymore. That torque figure is the headline number for towing.

TowingUp to 9,520 lbs

Tow rating up to ~9,000-9,520 lbs

Depending on configuration. Bring boat and toy-hauler owners the actual trailer weight and we'll match a trim and rear-axle setup to it, not just quote the max.

TrimsSR5 to Capstone

SR5, Limited, Platinum, TRD Pro, Capstone

TRD Pro is the off-road build with Fox shocks and a front skid plate; Capstone is the leather-and-walnut flagship. Most National City families land on Limited or Platinum.

Third rowNow adjustable

A third row that finally adjusts

Sliding third-row seat plus an available adjustable cargo shelf, addressing the single biggest complaint we used to hear about the old model's cramped way-back.

Safety and the spec sheet, plain English

Every 2025 Sequoia carries standard Toyota Safety Sense 3.0 across the lineup: pre-collision with pedestrian detection, lane-departure assist, adaptive cruise, and road-sign assist baked in, not optional. The i-FORCE MAX setup is a self-charging hybrid, so you never plug it in. For the official window-sticker fuel economy by trim and drivetrain, fueleconomy.gov is the source we point shoppers to. The hybrid changed those numbers from the prior generation, and we'd rather you see the EPA figure than trust a round number.

03The honest catch

Who should not buy a Sequoia.

We would rather lose a sale than put you in the wrong vehicle, so here's the part most dealers skip.

The Sequoia is big, genuinely big. It's a body-on-frame, truck-based SUV, which means it tows and hauls like a champion but parks and sips fuel like the truck it's related to. If your day is mostly two kids and a Target run, and you're not towing anything, a Highlander or Grand Highlander will save you money up front, fit your garage more easily, and return better real-world mileage. We have steered plenty of shoppers from the Sequoia down to a Grand Highlander when the towing need wasn't actually there, and they thanked us later.

Buy the Sequoia when you genuinely need full-size capability. Buy it for the right reason and it's hard to beat.

Buy the Sequoia when you genuinely need full-size capability: a trailer, a boat, eight occupants on the regular, or a long highway tow up the grade out of San Diego. Buy it because it looks imposing in the driveway and you may find yourself wishing for something smaller every time you parallel park on National City Blvd. We'll tell you that across the desk, before you sign.

Sequoia vs. the cross-shop.

Most full-size SUV shoppers in the South Bay weigh the Sequoia against a few specific rivals. Here's how we frame it honestly at the desk.

DomesticTahoe / Yukon

Sequoia vs. Chevrolet Tahoe / GMC Yukon

Roomier cargo and an available diesel on the domestics; the Sequoia counters with standard-hybrid efficiency and Toyota's resale and reliability track record. If you keep vehicles eight-plus years, that resale gap matters.

In-houseGrand Highlander

Sequoia vs. Toyota Grand Highlander

Lighter, more efficient, easier to live with daily, but it's a unibody crossover. It can't match the Sequoia's tow rating or body-on-frame durability.

PlatformLand Cruiser

Sequoia vs. Land Cruiser

Same platform family, very different mission. The Sequoia is the three-row family hauler; the Land Cruiser is the two-or-three-row trail tool. If you need eight seats, the Sequoia wins.

08Sequoia buyer questions

Frequently asked questions about the 2025 Toyota Sequoia.

Does Dalton Toyota National City have the 2025 Sequoia in stock?

Inventory changes often, and the redesigned Sequoia moves quickly. The fastest way to see which 2025 Sequoia trims are available right now, and which are inbound, is to check current stock through toyotadalton.com or visit us at 2400 National City Blvd.

Is the 2025 Sequoia a hybrid?

Yes, and it's the only option. Every 2025 Sequoia uses Toyota's i-FORCE MAX hybrid powertrain, a twin-turbo 3.4L V6 with an electric motor, rated at a combined 437 horsepower and 583 lb-ft of torque. There is no gas-only version.

It is a self-charging hybrid, so you never plug it in. For the EPA fuel-economy figures by trim, see fueleconomy.gov.

How much can the 2025 Sequoia tow?

Depending on configuration, the Sequoia is rated to tow up to roughly 9,000–9,520 lbs. If towing is your priority, bring us the actual weight of your trailer or boat and we'll match a trim and setup to it rather than quoting only the maximum.

Can I test drive a 2025 Sequoia?

Yes. Reach out through toyotadalton.com to set up a time, and we'll have a Sequoia ready when you arrive, fueled, with the third row and tow features ready to demonstrate.

Should I buy a Sequoia or a Grand Highlander?

It depends on whether you tow. If you regularly pull a trailer or boat or need genuine eight-passenger, body-on-frame capability, the Sequoia is the right call.

If you don't tow and mostly do school runs and errands, the Grand Highlander is lighter, more efficient, and easier to park, and we'll happily steer you to it. We'd rather you be in the right vehicle.

Can I trade in my current vehicle toward a Sequoia?

Absolutely. We appraise trade-ins as part of the buying process so you can see exactly how it affects your numbers. It helps to check your own KBB (kbb.com) or Edmunds (edmunds.com) estimate first so you can compare. Start the conversation at toyotadalton.com.

What maintenance does a Sequoia need?

Toyota's recommended schedule calls for basic service roughly every 5,000 miles or six months: oil, tire rotation, and inspection. Our service department follows Toyota's published maintenance schedule and will tell you what's genuinely due versus what can wait.
